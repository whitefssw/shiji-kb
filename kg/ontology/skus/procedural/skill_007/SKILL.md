# Nine Provinces Administrative System

A framework for organizing territory into administrative units with standardized assessment and tribute collection.

## Overview
This procedure divides territory into provinces (州), each with specific soil classifications, tax rates, and tribute obligations.

## Steps

1. **Province Delineation**
   - Define boundaries based on natural features (rivers, mountains)
   - Name each province (e.g., 冀州, 沇州, 青州, 徐州, 扬州, 荆州, 豫州, 梁州, 雍州)

2. **Soil Assessment**
   - Classify soil types (e.g., white soil 白壤, black soil 黑坟, red clay 赤埴坟)
   - Rank land quality (上上, 上中, 上下, 中上, etc.)
   - Determine agricultural potential

3. **Tax Classification**
   - Assign tax rates based on land productivity
   - Account for soil quality variations
   - Establish graduated payment schedules

4. **Tribute Specification**
   - Identify local products and resources
   - List specific tribute items per province (silk, salt, metals, timber, etc.)
   - Specify packaging requirements (篚 containers)

5. **Transportation Routes**
   - Map water routes for tribute transport
   - Identify key waterways and connections
   - Establish access points to main rivers

## Decision Points
- If soil quality varies within province, adjust tax rates accordingly
- If transportation is difficult, identify alternative routes

## Expected Outcomes
- Clear administrative boundaries
- Standardized taxation system
- Efficient tribute collection and transport

## Validation
- Verify that each province has a documented soil classification and corresponding tax rate
- Confirm that tribute items match each province's local products and resources
- Check that water transportation routes connect each province to the central collection points
