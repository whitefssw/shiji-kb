# Han-Xiongnu Heqin (和亲) Diplomacy Procedure

## Overview
The Heqin system established peace through marriage alliances, tribute payments, and mutual non-aggression agreements between Han China and the Xiongnu confederacy.

## Prerequisites
- Recognition of mutual benefit in avoiding war
- Willingness to exchange diplomatic correspondence
- Resources for annual tribute payments

## Steps

### 1. Initial Negotiation
- Exchange of diplomatic letters
- Agreement on terms of peace
- Recognition of territorial boundaries

### 2. Marriage Alliance
- Han sends imperial princess or noblewoman as bride to Shanyu
- Woman becomes Yanzhi (阏氏 - consort) to Xiongnu ruler

### 3. Tribute Agreement
- Annual payments from Han to Xiongnu:
  - Silk fabrics (絮缯)
  - Wine and grain (酒米)
  - Food supplies (食物)
  - Gold and silk (金帛丝絮)

### 4. Border Protocols
- Establish clear territorial boundaries
- **长城以北，引弓之国，受命单于** (North of Great Wall: bow-drawing peoples under Shanyu)
- **长城以内，冠带之室，朕亦制之** (Within Great Wall: civilized peoples under Han Emperor)

### 5. Trade Relations
- Open border markets (关市)
- Allow exchange of goods
- Maintain diplomatic communication channels

## Treaty Terms (Example)
- "匈奴无入塞，汉无出塞" (Xiongnu do not enter the passes, Han do not exit)
- "犯约者杀之" (Violators of treaty shall be killed)
- Return of fugitives and defectors

## Expected Outcomes
- Reduced border conflicts
- Stable diplomatic relations
- Economic exchange through border markets
- Temporary peace allowing both sides to recover

## Verification
- Confirm all treaty terms are formally agreed upon by both parties, including border boundaries and non-aggression clauses
- Validate that annual tribute items (絮缯, 酒米, 金帛丝絮) are delivered on schedule
- Check that border markets (关市) are operating and diplomatic communication channels remain active
