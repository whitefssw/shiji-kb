# Governance Approach Selection

## Overview
Framework for choosing between transformative and adaptive governance approaches, illustrated by the contrast between Lu and Qi.

## Two Approaches

### Transformative Approach (Bo Qin's Method)
- Change existing customs
- Reform established rituals
- Implement three-year mourning period
- **Timeline**: 3 years before reporting results
- **Risk**: May create distance from the people

### Adaptive Approach (Tai Gong's Method)
- Simplify ruler-subject rituals
- Follow existing customs
- Work within local practices
- **Timeline**: 5 months before reporting results
- **Risk**: May lose some control over transformation

## Decision Criteria

1. **Urgency of Results**: If quick stabilization is needed, choose adaptive approach
2. **Cultural Distance**: If local customs align with your goals, adapt; if not, transform
3. **Long-term Goals**: Consider whether integration or transformation serves better

## Key Principle
"Government that is not simple and easy will not be close to the people; if it is level and easy and close to the people, the people will surely return to it."

## Expected Outcomes
- Adaptive approach: Faster results, closer relationship with populace
- Transformative approach: Slower results, more thorough cultural change

## Warning
Overly complex or rigid governance creates distance from the people and may lead to subordination to more adaptive competitors.

## Validation
1. Confirm the decision criteria were properly evaluated: urgency of results, cultural distance, and long-term goals
2. Verify the chosen approach matches conditions — adaptive for urgent stabilization, transformative only when local customs fundamentally conflict with goals
3. Check the outcome: governance is 简 and 易 (simple and easy), the people feel close (民近), and the populace is returning rather than departing
