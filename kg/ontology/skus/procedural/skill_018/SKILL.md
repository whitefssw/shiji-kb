# Crisis Management Protocol (危机应对程序)

Procedures for managing political crises, drawn from Zhou dynasty examples.

## Natural Disaster Response

### Earthquake Protocol (伯阳甫's Analysis)

1. **Interpret the Signs**
   - "阳伏而不能出，阴迫而不能蒸" (Yang trapped, Yin compressed)
   - Recognize as warning of regime failure

2. **Historical Pattern Recognition**
   - "昔伊、洛竭而夏亡" (Yi and Luo dried up, Xia fell)
   - "河竭而商亡" (Yellow River dried up, Shang fell)
   - Apply lessons to current situation

3. **Timeline Assessment**
   - "国亡不过十年，数之纪也" (State falls within 10 years)
   - Use timeline to prioritize reforms

## Rebellion Management

### Prevention Phase

1. **Monitor Public Sentiment**
   - "国人谤王" (People criticize the king)
   - Do not suppress speech

2. **Allow Free Expression** (召公's Method)
   - "防民之口，甚於防水" (Blocking people's mouths is worse than blocking water)
   - "为水者决之使导，为民者宣之使言" (Guide water, let people speak)

3. **Establish Feedback Channels**
   - 公卿献诗
   - 瞽献曲
   - 史献书
   - 师箴
   - 庶人传语
   - 近臣尽规
   - 亲戚补察

### Active Rebellion Response

1. **Do Not Escalate**
   - Avoid harsh crackdowns
   - Address underlying grievances

2. **Protect Key Personnel**
   - Example: 召公 hiding crown prince
   - "以其子代王太子" (Substituted his own son)

3. **Accept Temporary Exile**
   - 厉王出奔於彘
   - Wait for restoration opportunity

## Regency Protocol (周公's Example)

1. **When Ruler is Incapacitated**
   - Establish regency: "摄行政当国"
   - Maintain stability: "以朝诸侯"

2. **Handle Family Opposition**
   - 管叔、蔡叔作乱
   - Suppress firmly but justly
   - "伐诛武庚、管叔，放蔡叔"

3. **Return Power Appropriately**
   - "成王长，周公反政成王"
   - Step back when legitimate ruler ready

## Expected Outcomes
- Crisis contained without permanent damage
- Legitimate succession preserved
- Institutional continuity maintained

## Validation
- Verify that feedback channels (公卿献诗, 瞽献曲, 史献书, etc.) are established and functioning before crisis escalates
- Confirm that historical pattern analysis was applied to interpret natural disaster signs and set reform timelines
- Check that regency protocols include a defined endpoint for returning power to the legitimate ruler when ready
