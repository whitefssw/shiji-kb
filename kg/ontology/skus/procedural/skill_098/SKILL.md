# Minister Selection Through Meritocracy

A process for recommending and selecting officials based on capability rather than favoritism.

## Overview
Selecting ministers should prioritize ability over personal relationships to ensure effective governance.

## Recommendation Principles

### External Recommendations
- Do not exclude personal enemies if they are capable
- Evaluate based on track record and skills
- Prioritize state interest over personal feelings

### Internal Recommendations
- Do not exclude relatives if they are capable
- Avoid appearance of nepotism by documenting merit
- Ensure transparent evaluation criteria

## Selection Process

1. **Define Position Requirements**
   - Specific skills needed
   - Experience required
   - Character traits essential

2. **Gather Candidates**
   - Accept recommendations from multiple sources
   - Consider both established and emerging talents

3. **Evaluate Candidates**
   - Review past performance
   - Assess character and judgment
   - Test specific competencies

4. **Check for Conflicts**
   - Ensure no disqualifying relationships
   - Verify loyalty to state

5. **Make Selection**
   - Choose based on merit ranking
   - Document reasoning
   - Announce appointment publicly

## Example
Qi Xi recommended his personal enemy Jie Hu for a position, then his own son Qi Wu for another position, demonstrating impartial merit-based selection.

## Expected Outcome
Competent officials selected through fair process, earning public trust and effective governance.

## Verification
1. Confirm that candidates were evaluated on documented merit criteria (skills, experience, character) rather than personal relationships alone.
2. Validate that both external candidates (including former adversaries) and internal candidates (including relatives) were considered without bias.
3. Check that the selection reasoning was documented and announced publicly to maintain transparency and trust.
