# Escape from Captivity

## Overview
A method for escaping enemy capture by feigning death or injury, then seizing an opportunity to capture enemy resources and flee.

## Steps

1. **Feign injury or death**: Pretend to be more severely wounded or dead than actual condition

2. **Observe surroundings**: While appearing incapacitated, carefully observe guards, horses, weapons, and terrain

3. **Identify opportunity**: Watch for moments when guards are distracted or a suitable target (horse, weapon) is nearby

4. **Act suddenly**: Without warning, spring into action
   - Mount a nearby horse
   - Push off or disable any rider
   - Seize available weapons

5. **Flee immediately**: Ride hard in the direction of safety

6. **Defend during pursuit**: Use captured weapons to engage pursuers if necessary

7. **Reach friendly territory**: Navigate back to allied forces or safe territory

## Key Success Factors
- Patience in waiting for the right moment
- Convincing performance of injury
- Quick, decisive action when opportunity arises
- Ability to use enemy equipment

## Expected Outcomes
- Successful escape from captivity
- Possible elimination of pursuers
- Return to friendly forces

## Verification
- Confirm the feigned injury is convincing enough that guards have reduced their vigilance
- Validate that an escape route and mount have been identified before committing to action
- Check that friendly territory has been reached and pursuers are no longer a threat before standing down
