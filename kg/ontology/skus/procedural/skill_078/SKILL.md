# Military Oath and Discipline Procedure

## Overview
Procedure for issuing military proclamations and establishing discipline, based on Bo Qin's oath at Bi.

## Steps

1. **Issue the Proclamation**
   - Gather the troops
   - Deliver formal oath addressing all soldiers

2. **Equipment Inspection Orders**
   - Command soldiers to properly arrange armor and helmets
   - Prohibit improper maintenance or damage to equipment

3. **Rules of Conduct**
   - Prohibit pursuing stray horses, cattle, or fleeing servants
   - Order return of lost property to rightful owners
   - Prohibit looting, robbery, and trespassing

4. **Logistics Requirements**
   - Specify supply contributions from each district
   - List required items: fodder, firewood, dry provisions, construction materials
   - Set clear deadlines for delivery

5. **Enforcement**
   - State clear consequences for non-compliance
   - Specify severe penalties for violations

## Sample Proclamation Elements
- "Arrange your armor and helmets, do not be negligent"
- "Do not pursue stray livestock or fleeing servants"
- "Respectfully return them"
- "Do not loot or trespass"
- "Prepare your supplies without fail"
- "Those who do not meet the deadline will face severe punishment"

## Expected Outcomes
- Disciplined military force
- Clear understanding of rules and consequences
- Proper logistical preparation for campaign

## Validation
1. Confirm all troops have received the proclamation and understand the rules of conduct — no looting, no trespassing, return of lost property
2. Verify that supply contributions from each district are specified with clear deadlines and all required items (fodder, firewood, provisions, materials) are accounted for
3. Check that enforcement penalties are clearly stated and known to all — consequences for non-compliance are unambiguous
