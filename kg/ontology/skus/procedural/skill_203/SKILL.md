## Overview
A framework for assessing the viability of military campaigns against nomadic or highly mobile enemies, weighing costs against potential gains.

## Assessment Criteria
1. **Distance Analysis**: Evaluate the logistics of fighting at a distance
   - "千里而战，兵不获利" (Fighting at a thousand li, troops gain no advantage)

2. **Enemy Characteristics**: Assess the adversary's nature
   - Mobility: "迁徙鸟举" (migrate like birds taking flight)
   - Unpredictability: "怀禽兽之心" (harboring the heart of wild beasts)
   - Difficulty to control: "难得而制" (hard to capture and control)

3. **Territory Value**: Evaluate what would be gained
   - Land: "得其地不足以为广" (gaining their land is insufficient for expansion)
   - Population: "有其众不足以为彊" (possessing their people is insufficient for strength)

4. **Force Degradation**: Consider how your forces weaken over distance
   - "人马罢" (men and horses become exhausted)
   - Enemy fights fresh against your depleted forces

5. **Diminishing Returns**: Apply the crossbow metaphor
   - "彊弩之极，矢不能穿鲁缟" (At its limit, a strong crossbow's arrow cannot pierce fine silk)
   - Initial strength degrades over distance and time

## Decision Framework
- If enemy is mobile, territory has low value, and distance is great → favor diplomacy/alliance
- If engagement is unavoidable → ensure close-range, fresh forces

## Expected Outcomes
- Avoid costly campaigns with low probability of success
- Preserve military resources for more favorable engagements

## Verification
- Confirm all five assessment criteria (distance, enemy characteristics, territory value, force degradation, diminishing returns) have been evaluated
- Validate that the decision framework conclusion aligns with the evidence gathered
- Check that alternative options (diplomacy, alliance) were considered before recommending military action
