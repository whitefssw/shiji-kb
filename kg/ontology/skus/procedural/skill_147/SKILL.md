# Strategic Cost-Benefit Analysis

Based on Hu Yan's advice against attacking Pu.

## Overview
Before attacking a target, analyze whether success helps or harms your strategic position.

## Steps

1. **Identify the Target's Strategic Value**
   - What does the target provide to whom?
   - Example: Pu is vital to Wei's independence

2. **Analyze Consequences of Success**
   - If you capture the target, who benefits?
   - Will success strengthen a rival?
   - Example: Capturing Pu would force Wei to submit to your rival (Wei)

3. **Evaluate Secondary Effects**
   - How will power balance shift?
   - Will a weakened party become stronger through consolidation?

4. **Consider Ruler's Perception**
   - Will this action be seen as helping or harming the state?
   - Ruler will judge based on outcomes, not intentions

5. **Recommend Alternative**
   - If action harms your interests, propose alternative
   - Find way to gain credit without negative consequences

## Decision Framework
- If action helps enemy more than you: Do not proceed
- If action strengthens rival state: Do not proceed
- If ruler will blame you for consequences: Do not proceed

## Expected Outcomes
- Avoid strategic mistakes
- Maintain ruler's favor
- Prevent unintended strengthening of rivals

## Validation
1. Map the full chain of consequences: target capture leads to which power shift, which forces which consolidation, which benefits whom — if the chain ends at a rival's gain, do not proceed
2. Verify the alternative proposal genuinely serves state interests and is not merely a face-saving inaction plan
3. Confirm the ruler's perception has been considered — will the ruler credit you for wisdom in restraint, or blame you for missed opportunity?
