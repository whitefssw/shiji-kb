# Judicial Investigation Procedure

## Overview
Traditional Chinese legal investigation framework emphasizing thorough examination, proportional punishment, and protection of the innocent.

## Investigation Principles

### 1. Case Assessment
- "过听杀人，自拘当死" (If wrongly convicted someone to death, submit oneself for execution)
- Officials bear responsibility for errors
- "失刑则刑，失死则死" (Wrong punishment = receive punishment; wrong death = accept death)

### 2. Evidence Gathering
- Examine all witnesses
- Cross-reference testimonies
- "傅其罪下吏" (Cannot transfer blame to subordinates)

### 3. Hierarchy of Responsibility
- "臣居官为长，不与吏让位" (As head official, don't yield position to subordinates)
- "受禄为多，不与下分利" (Receive more salary, don't share credit with subordinates)
- Therefore: Accept full responsibility for errors

## Case Processing

### For Treason/Rebellion Cases
1. Investigate all connections
2. "穷根本" (Trace to the root)
3. Document all accomplices
4. Consider if some were coerced vs. willing

### For Property Crimes
1. Determine value of stolen goods
2. Assess if violence was used
3. Consider circumstances (need vs. greed)

### For Official Misconduct
1. Compare actions to established law
2. Examine intent vs. negligence
3. Consider past service record

## Punishment Guidelines
- Proportional to offense
- Consider social status appropriately
- "不避贵戚" (Don't spare the powerful)
- "不私其父" (Don't favor one's own father)

## Ethical Standards
- Judge must be willing to accept same punishment if wrong
- Cannot use position to escape accountability
- Death before dishonor of the law

## Expected Outcomes
- Public trust in legal system
- Deterrence of crime
- Rehabilitation where possible
- Justice seen to be done

## Verification
- Confirm the investigating official has accepted personal accountability for potential errors (失刑则刑, 失死则死)
- Validate that evidence was gathered from multiple witnesses with cross-referenced testimonies
- Check that punishment is proportional to the offense and applied impartially regardless of the accused's social status (不避贵戚)
