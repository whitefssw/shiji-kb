# Nine Virtues (九德) Leadership Assessment

A framework for evaluating and developing leadership character based on complementary virtues.

## Overview
Gao Yao's nine virtues provide a balanced approach to assessing leadership potential and character development.

## The Nine Virtues

1. **宽而栗** - Broad-minded yet principled
2. **柔而立** - Gentle yet firm in standing
3. **愿而共** - Sincere yet respectful
4. **治而敬** - Orderly yet reverent
5. **扰而毅** - Adaptable yet resolute
6. **直而温** - Upright yet warm
7. **简而廉** - Simple yet incorruptible
8. **刚而实** - Strong yet substantive
9. **彊而义** - Powerful yet righteous

## Application Guidelines

### For Daily Practice
- Practice three virtues daily → Qualify for household management (有家)
- Practice six virtues with dedication → Qualify for state governance (有国)
- Master all nine virtues → Qualify for highest office

### Assessment Process

1. **Observe Behavior**
   - Watch how the person handles various situations
   - Note balance between complementary traits

2. **Evaluate Consistency**
   - Check if virtues are demonstrated regularly (有常)
   - Assess long-term character stability

3. **Match to Position**
   - Higher positions require more virtues
   - Consider specific virtues needed for role

4. **Provide Development**
   - Guide cultivation of missing virtues
   - Create opportunities to practice

## Warning Signs
- Do not appoint those lacking virtue to positions
- Wrong appointments create chaos (乱天事)
- Match consequences to transgressions (five punishments for five crimes)

## Expected Outcomes
- Officials of proven character
- Balanced leadership team
- Just administration

## Validation
- Verify that each candidate has been assessed against all nine virtues with documented observations
- Confirm that the number of consistently demonstrated virtues matches the level of position assigned (3 for household, 6 for state, 9 for highest office)
- Check that no candidate lacking demonstrated virtue consistency (有常) has been appointed to a position of authority
