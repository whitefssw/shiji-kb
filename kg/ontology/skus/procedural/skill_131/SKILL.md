# Ancient Leadership Succession Protocol

## Overview
A method for legitimate power transfer based on ancient Chinese sage king practices (Yao, Shun, Yu), emphasizing merit-based selection and extensive testing before formal transition.

## Steps

1. **Gather Recommendations**
   - Solicit input from ministers and regional officials (岳牧)
   - Identify potential successors through collective endorsement

2. **Test in Position**
   - Place candidate in official role
   - Allow extended period of service (数十年 - decades)

3. **Evaluate Performance**
   - Assess actual accomplishments and utility (功用既兴)
   - Verify capability through demonstrated results

4. **Formal Transfer**
   - Only after proven success, formally grant governing authority
   - Recognize the gravity of transferring "heavy instruments of state" (天下重器)

## Decision Points
- Do not proceed if candidate lacks broad support from officials
- Do not transfer power until performance is verified over time

## Expected Outcomes
- Legitimate, stable leadership transition
- Reduced risk of unsuitable successors
- Public confidence in the succession process

## Validation
1. Confirm the candidate received broad endorsement from ministers and regional officials (岳牧), not just a single faction's support
2. Verify the testing period produced measurable accomplishments (功用既兴) — not merely symbolic presence in office
3. Assess whether the transfer acknowledges the gravity of 天下重器 (heavy instruments of state) through proper ceremony and public announcement
