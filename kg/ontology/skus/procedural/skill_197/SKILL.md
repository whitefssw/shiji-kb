## Overview
This procedure outlines how to respond when facing public criticism or accusations, particularly in political or organizational contexts. The approach emphasizes humility and self-reflection over counter-attack.

## Steps
1. **When criticized, do not immediately retaliate** - Avoid responding with counter-accusations or defensive arguments
2. **Remove symbols of authority** - Take off ceremonial items (traditionally: remove hat and surrender official seal)
3. **Issue a humble acknowledgment** - State: "I have been fortunate to serve in my position due to connections, but I am truly not worthy of this role. The accusations against me are correct."
4. **Offer resignation** - Propose to step down from the position
5. **Allow the superior to decide** - This approach typically results in:
   - The superior appreciating your humility and not dismissing you
   - The accuser feeling ashamed and potentially withdrawing

## Expected Outcomes
- Preservation of reputation through demonstrated humility
- Superior's respect for your self-awareness and grace
- Accuser loses moral high ground
- Avoids appearing petty or lacking dignity

## Warning
Retaliating when criticized makes one appear like a common merchant or quarreling woman, lacking proper dignity and perspective.

## Verification
1. Confirm that the initial response avoided all counter-accusations and defensive arguments.
2. Validate that symbols of authority were surrendered and a humble acknowledgment was issued before the superior's decision.
3. Check that the accuser has lost moral high ground as a result of your demonstrated humility, rather than gaining further ammunition.
