# Strategic Patience Warfare

Based on Wang Jian's conquest of Chu with 600,000 troops.

## Overview
When commanding a large force against a determined enemy, use patience and comfort to maintain readiness while waiting for enemy to make mistakes.

## Steps

1. **Gather Overwhelming Force**
   - Request more troops than seemingly necessary
   - Do not proceed with inadequate forces
   - Accept criticism of "cowardice" rather than risk defeat

2. **Establish Defensive Position**
   - Build fortifications upon arrival
   - Refuse all challenges to battle
   - Let enemy exhaust themselves trying to provoke engagement

3. **Maintain Troop Welfare**
   - Provide excellent food and rest
   - Allow recreational activities
   - Personally share conditions with soldiers

4. **Monitor Readiness**
   - Watch for signs of boredom and energy
   - When soldiers start "throwing stones and jumping distances" they are ready
   - High morale indicates combat readiness

5. **Strike at Withdrawal**
   - Enemy will eventually tire and withdraw
   - Pursue immediately with full force
   - Destroy enemy while they are in motion

## Expected Outcomes
- Preserved force strength
- High morale at moment of attack
- Enemy caught in vulnerable transition

## Validation
1. Monitor troop welfare indicators — when soldiers begin recreational activities ("throwing stones and jumping distances"), combat readiness is high; continued lethargy signals morale problems
2. Verify the enemy has genuinely begun withdrawal versus executing a feigned retreat to draw you from fortifications
3. Confirm pursuit forces are fully committed once the strike order is given — half-hearted pursuit allows enemy to regroup
