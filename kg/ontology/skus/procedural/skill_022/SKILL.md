# Implement Legal Reform

## Overview
A procedure for implementing comprehensive legal reforms, including establishing rule of law, agricultural incentives, and military merit systems. Based on Shang Yang's reforms in Qin.

## Steps

1. **Assess Current State**
   - Identify weaknesses: internal disorder, external threats, lost territories
   - Recognize when current systems are failing

2. **Issue Public Call for Talent**
   - Announce willingness to appoint and reward those with effective strategies
   - Offer high positions and land to capable reformers

3. **Debate Reform Proposals**
   - Allow open discussion of proposed changes
   - Address objections from traditionalists
   - Secure ruler's support for implementation

4. **Implement Core Reforms**
   - **Legal System**: Establish clear laws and punishments
   - **Agriculture**: Incentivize farming and land cultivation
   - **Military**: Create merit-based reward system for battlefield achievements

5. **Enforce Laws Equally**
   - Apply laws to all ranks including nobility
   - When high-ranking individuals violate laws, punish their guardians or representatives
   - Demonstrate that no one is above the law

6. **Allow Adjustment Period**
   - Expect initial resistance and hardship
   - Persist through early difficulties
   - Benefits will become apparent over time

7. **Institutionalize Changes**
   - Appoint reformers to high positions
   - Continue expanding and refining the system

## Decision Points
- If traditionalists object, remind them that past failures prove old methods don't work
- If nobility resists, enforce laws through their representatives

## Expected Outcomes
- Strengthened agricultural base
- Motivated military forces
- Enhanced state power and territorial expansion

## Validation
- Verify that reform proposals were openly debated and the ruler's explicit support was secured before implementation
- Confirm that laws are being applied equally across all ranks, including nobility, with documented enforcement cases
- Check that agricultural productivity and military recruitment show measurable improvement after the adjustment period
