# Imperial Inspection Tour Protocol

A five-year cycle of systematic inspection tours covering the four cardinal directions of the realm.

## Overview
The emperor conducts inspection tours to verify governance, standardize measurements, perform rituals, and assess local conditions throughout the territory.

## Steps

1. **Preparation**
   - Select auspicious days for departure
   - Gather the five jade tablets (五瑞) as credentials
   - Prepare ritual items and gifts

2. **Eastern Tour (Second Month)**
   - Travel to Dai Zong (Mount Tai)
   - Perform fire ritual (祡) to heaven
   - Conduct mountain and river rituals in order
   - Meet with eastern regional leaders
   - Standardize: calendar, measurements, weights
   - Review: five rituals, five jades, three silks, two live animals, one dead animal as gifts

3. **Southern Tour (Fifth Month)**
   - Follow same protocol as eastern tour
   - Assess southern governance
   - Perform regional rituals

4. **Western Tour (Eighth Month)**
   - Follow same protocol as eastern tour
   - Assess western governance
   - Perform regional rituals

5. **Northern Tour (Eleventh Month)**
   - Follow same protocol as eastern tour
   - Assess northern governance
   - Perform regional rituals

6. **Return and Report**
   - Return to ancestral temple
   - Perform special bull sacrifice
   - Document findings and decisions

7. **Audience Period**
   - Regional lords attend court for four years
   - Receive verbal instructions
   - Demonstrate achievements
   - Receive rewards (chariots, clothing) based on merit

## Cycle Schedule
- Year 1: Imperial tour
- Years 2-5: Lords attend court
- Repeat cycle

## Expected Outcomes
- Unified standards across realm
- Direct knowledge of regional conditions
- Strengthened central authority
- Accountable regional governance

## Validation
- Verify that calendars, weights, and measurements are consistent across all four regions after the tour
- Confirm that each regional leader has received and returned the five jade tablets (五瑞)
- Check that findings and decisions from the tour are documented and reported at the ancestral temple
