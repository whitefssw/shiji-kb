## Overview
A strategic framework for planning leadership change through targeted action, as demonstrated in Prince Guang's (later King Helü) removal of King Liao.

## Steps

1. **Establish Legitimacy Claim**
   - Document your rightful position in succession
   - Build narrative for why you should lead

2. **Recruit Capable Agents**
   - Identify talented individuals seeking opportunity
   - Provide them support and position
   - Build loyalty through patronage

3. **Identify Optimal Timing**
   - Wait for external distractions (military campaigns, foreign threats)
   - Ensure target's key supporters are absent
   - Confirm internal defenses are weakened

4. **Select Appropriate Method**
   - Choose approach suited to target's habits
   - Conceal weapons in everyday items
   - Plan for close-quarters action

5. **Establish Operational Security**
   - Station forces in concealed locations
   - Create diversion or pretext for gathering
   - Maintain cover until moment of action

6. **Execute and Consolidate**
   - Act decisively when opportunity presents
   - Immediately assume leadership position
   - Honor and reward those who assisted

## Key Conditions for Success
- Target's key military leaders are engaged elsewhere
- Target's family supporters are absent
- Internal government lacks strong opposition figures
- External threats create distraction

## Expected Outcomes
- Successful transition of power
- Minimal resistance due to timing
- Legitimacy through rapid consolidation

## Validation
1. Confirm the legitimacy claim is documented and the succession narrative is established before action
2. Verify timing conditions are optimal: target's key military leaders and family supporters are absent, external threats create distraction
3. Check that consolidation is immediate — leadership is assumed, loyal agents are rewarded, and internal opposition is neutralized
