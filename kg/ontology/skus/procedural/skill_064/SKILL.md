## Overview
A method for assessing the health and trajectory of foreign states through diplomatic visits, as demonstrated by Ji Zha's missions to various states.

## Steps

1. **Assess Political Leadership**
   - Observe the ruling class behavior
   - Note concentration of power trends
   - Identify virtuous individuals in government

2. **Evaluate Imminent Dangers**
   - Look for signs of political instability
   - Identify those who may face calamity
   - Warn appropriate individuals discretely

3. **Provide Strategic Advice**
   - Recommend divestment of power/territory when danger looms
   - Advise on self-preservation strategies
   - Suggest appropriate cautionary measures

4. **Identify Safe Havens**
   - Look for states with many virtuous men (君子)
   - Assess which states will avoid disaster
   - Note concentration of capable leadership

## Examples from Practice
- In Qi: Advised Yan Pingzhong to give up territory and political power to avoid disaster
- In Zheng: Warned Zichan of coming turmoil and advised governing with caution and ritual
- In Wei: Identified many virtuous men, predicted safety from disaster
- In Jin: Predicted power consolidation in three families; advised Shuxiang on self-preservation

## Decision Points
- If leadership is extravagant and power is consolidating, predict political upheaval
- If virtuous individuals abound, predict stability
- If ruling class is complacent while danger approaches, warn of imminent threat

## Expected Outcomes
- Accurate prediction of political changes
- Ability to advise allies on avoiding disaster
- Recognition of which states will rise or fall

## Validation
1. Confirm political leadership has been assessed for power concentration trends and signs of instability
2. Verify that at-risk individuals have been warned discreetly with specific self-preservation advice (divestment of power/territory)
3. Check that states with many virtuous men (君子) are identified as safe havens and those with extravagant, complacent leadership are flagged for upheaval
