# High-Stakes Negotiation Tactics

## Overview
A procedure for breaking negotiation deadlocks when time is critical and the opposing party is stalling or dismissive.

## Steps

1. **Assess the situation** - Recognize when negotiations have stalled despite clear mutual interest
2. **Enter forcefully** - Approach directly with urgency, bypassing normal protocol
3. **Frame the simplicity** - State that the decision should be simple and quick
4. **Challenge dismissiveness** - If dismissed as subordinate, physically advance while maintaining composure
5. **Neutralize power imbalance** - Point out that in close proximity, the other party's external power is irrelevant
6. **Reframe the narrative** - Show how the agreement benefits THEM, not just you
7. **Invoke historical precedent** - Reference examples of small forces achieving great things through determination
8. **Remind of past humiliations** - Connect current inaction to historical grievances that demand response
9. **Demand immediate commitment** - Ask for explicit agreement
10. **Formalize immediately** - Conduct the ritual (blood oath, signing) on the spot before doubts resurface

## Critical Techniques
- Physical proximity reduces psychological distance
- Reframe the deal as serving their interests
- Use shame and honor as motivators
- Do not leave without explicit commitment

## Expected Outcome
Immediate decision, formalized agreement, successful conclusion of stalled negotiations

## Validation
1. Confirm the reframing genuinely shows mutual benefit — if the deal only serves your side, the counterparty will resent the coercion and may renege later
2. Verify the formalization was completed on the spot (blood oath, signing, exchange) before the counterparty has time to reconsider — delay defeats the purpose
3. Assess whether shame and honor motivators were calibrated correctly — too much shame creates enemies; too little fails to motivate action
