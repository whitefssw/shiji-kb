# Military Campaign Planning

## Overview
Classical Chinese military planning methodology drawn from historical campaigns, emphasizing logistics, timing, and strategic positioning.

## Pre-Campaign Assessment

### 1. Intelligence Gathering
- Know enemy strength and disposition
- Assess terrain advantages
- "知水草处" (Know water and pasture locations)

### 2. Resource Planning
- Calculate provisions needed
- Plan supply lines
- Example: 60,000 troops require extensive logistics
- "负私从者不与" (Personal servants not counted in official numbers)

### 3. Alliance Coordination
- Secure commitments from allies
- Example: Request 2,000 cavalry from allies
- Have contingency if allies "持两端" (hesitate/hold both sides)

## Campaign Execution

### Strategic Positioning
1. Control key passes:
   - "绝成皋之口，天下不通" (Block Chenggao pass, cut off the realm)
2. Secure water sources
3. Establish supply depots

### Tactical Decisions
- "围其城，攻之四十馀日" (Besiege cities, attack for 40+ days if needed)
- Cut enemy resources: "决其水源" (Divert their water source)
- Accept surrender with conditions when advantageous

### Managing Difficult Terrain
- "道远多乏食" (Long routes lack food)
- "士卒不患战，患饥" (Soldiers fear hunger, not fighting)
- Plan for: "出其北有胡寇，出其南乏水草" (North: enemy raids; South: no water/grass)

## Post-Campaign

### Securing Victory
1. Install friendly rulers
2. Leave garrison forces
3. Establish administrative control
4. "置使者护田积粟" (Station officials to protect farmland and store grain)

### Reward Distribution
- Promote capable officers
- Distribute captured wealth
- Honor the fallen

## Expected Outcomes
- Territorial expansion
- Enemy neutralization
- Resource acquisition
- Strategic position secured

## Verification
- Confirm intelligence gathering covers enemy strength, terrain advantages, and water/pasture locations before campaign launch
- Validate that supply lines are calculated for the full troop count and contingencies exist for ally hesitation (持两端)
- Check that post-campaign steps (garrison forces, administrative control, farmland protection) are executed to secure the victory
