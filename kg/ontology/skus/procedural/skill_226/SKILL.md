## Overview
A political strategy to gradually weaken feudal lords by dividing their territories among all heirs rather than consolidating power with a single successor.

## Steps

1. **Identify the Target Structure**
   - Assess feudal lords with large territories and multiple sons
   - Note that only the primary heir traditionally inherits all land

2. **Implement the Decree**
   - Issue an imperial order allowing lords to divide territories among ALL sons
   - Frame it as an act of benevolence (µÄ©µü® - extending grace)
   - Each son receives a portion as a marquisate

3. **Execute the Division**
   - Lords must partition their lands
   - Create new smaller administrative units for each heir

4. **Monitor Results**
   - Territories become progressively smaller with each generation
   - No direct confrontation or forced confiscation needed
   - Formerly powerful states fragment into minor holdings

## Expected Outcomes
- Feudal lords grateful for imperial "benevolence"
- Large territories systematically divided and weakened
- Central authority strengthened without military action
- Prevents coordinated rebellion from powerful regional lords
