# Persuasion Through Implied Consequences

Based on 12-year-old Gan Luo's persuasion of Zhang Tang to accept a dangerous mission.

## Overview
Compare the target's situation to historical examples of those who refused orders and suffered consequences.

## Steps

1. **Establish Relative Authority**
   - Ask: "How does your achievement compare to [famous predecessor]?"
   - Get acknowledgment that target is less accomplished

2. **Compare Patrons**
   - Ask: "How does current patron's authority compare to previous era?"
   - Establish that current patron has equal or greater power

3. **Cite Consequence Example**
   - Reference someone who refused similar orders
   - Describe their fate clearly (exile, death)

4. **Draw Implicit Conclusion**
   - State: "I don't know where you will die"
   - Let target reach their own conclusion

5. **Allow Face-Saving Exit**
   - Let target agree through a subordinate
   - "I will go because of this child's words"

## Expected Outcomes
- Target agrees to take action
- Relationship preserved
- No direct confrontation required

## Validation
1. Confirm the historical analogy is accurate and the target recognizes the referenced predecessor's fate — an obscure or irrelevant example fails to create the intended pressure
2. Verify a face-saving exit was provided so the target can agree without appearing coerced ("I will go because of this child's words")
3. Check that the implied consequence was left implicit — making the threat explicit transforms persuasion into coercion and damages the relationship
