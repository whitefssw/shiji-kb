# Assessing Reward Justification

## Overview
A procedure for determining whether to accept honors or rewards by analyzing the true basis of your position and the implications of accepting.

## Analysis Steps

1. **Identify the basis of your current position** - Why were you given authority? (Ability, relationship, or lack of alternatives?)
2. **Identify the basis of your current rewards** - Why were you given wealth/fiefs? (Merit, relationship, or political necessity?)
3. **Assess the new reward's justification** - Did you actually contribute to the outcome being rewarded?
4. **Check for contradiction** - Does accepting create a logical inconsistency with how you obtained your position?
5. **Consider the intermediary's motives** - Does the person proposing the reward benefit regardless of outcome?

## Key Principle
If your position is based on relationship (not merit), accepting merit-based rewards creates a contradiction that undermines your legitimacy.

## Warning About Intermediaries
Those who propose rewards may:
- Claim credit if you accept ("I arranged this")
- Blame you if it fails
- Gain leverage over you

## Expected Outcome
Maintaining consistency, avoiding obligations, preserving long-term legitimacy

## Verification
1. Confirm that the basis of your position (merit vs. relationship) has been honestly identified before evaluating the reward.
2. Check whether accepting creates a logical contradiction between how you gained your position and the reward's stated justification.
3. Validate that intermediary motives have been assessed for hidden leverage or self-serving agendas.
