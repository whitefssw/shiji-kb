# Psychological Warfare Siege Defense

A comprehensive approach to defending a besieged city by manipulating enemy psychology and boosting defender morale.

## Phase 1: Divine Legitimacy
1. Create supernatural signs (e.g., birds gathering from ritual offerings)
2. Claim divine instruction and appoint a "divine advisor"
3. Issue commands in the name of the divine authority

## Phase 2: Manipulate Enemy Behavior
1. Spread rumors about what you fear (e.g., "we fear they will cut off our noses")
2. Enemy, thinking to demoralize you, does exactly that
3. This enrages defenders and strengthens resolve

## Phase 3: Feign Weakness
1. Send old people and women to man the walls
2. Send negotiators to discuss surrender terms
3. Bribe enemy commanders with personal appeals
4. Enemy becomes complacent

## Phase 4: The Surprise Attack
1. Prepare innovative weapons (e.g., fire cattle with swords on horns)
2. Attack at night with overwhelming noise and visual terror
3. Follow with infantry assault

## Key Principle
"Begin like a maiden, then strike like a fleeing rabbit" - lull the enemy into complacency before striking.

## Expected Outcomes
- Enemy morale shattered
- Defender morale maximized
- Successful breakout or relief

## Verification
1. Confirm that the feigned weakness phase has genuinely induced enemy complacency before launching the surprise attack.
2. Validate that innovative weapons and night-attack preparations are fully staged and tested before execution.
3. Ensure defender morale has been bolstered through the divine legitimacy and psychological manipulation phases before committing to the final assault.
