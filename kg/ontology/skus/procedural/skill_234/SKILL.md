# Governance Through Frugality Framework

## Overview
A governance philosophy establishing that national prosperity begins with enriching the people, and enriching the people depends on frugality in leadership.

## Core Principles

1. **Foundation of Governance**
   - "治国之道，富民为始" (The way of governing begins with enriching the people)
   - "富民之要，在于节俭" (The key to enriching the people lies in frugality)

2. **Role of Leadership Example**
   - "三公者，百寮之率，万民之表也" (The Three Excellencies are the leaders of all officials and models for all people)
   - "未有树直表而得曲影者也" (Never has a straight post cast a crooked shadow)
   - "子率而正，孰敢不正" (If you lead with rectitude, who would dare not be upright?)

3. **Social Benefits of Frugality**
   - Proper ordering of social hierarchy (尊卑之序得)
   - Strengthening of family bonds (骨肉之恩亲)
   - Elimination of disputes (争讼之原息)
   - Economic sufficiency (家给人足)
   - Reduced need for punishment (刑错之本)

## Implementation Steps

1. **Lead by Example**
   - High officials must demonstrate personal frugality
   - Avoid ostentation and excess

2. **Establish Clear Standards**
   - Define appropriate levels of consumption for each rank
   - Enforce through social pressure and official review

3. **Reward Frugal Conduct**
   - Recognize and promote officials who demonstrate austerity
   - Make frugality a criterion for advancement

4. **Discourage Excess**
   - Criticize ostentation publicly
   - Link luxury to moral failing

## Expected Outcomes
- Reduced government expenditure
- Lower tax burden on people
- Social harmony through reduced competition
- Stronger moral foundation for governance
- Lasting prosperity across generations

## Verification
- Confirm high officials are demonstrating personal frugality consistent with the standards they set
- Validate that consumption standards are defined and enforced for each rank level
- Check that frugal conduct is being recognized and rewarded in promotion decisions as a criterion for advancement
