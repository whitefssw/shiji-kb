# Five Punishments Judicial Procedure (五刑程序)

A systematic legal framework for determining appropriate punishments based on evidence verification levels.

## Overview
When adjudicating cases, officials must follow a graduated process of verification before applying punishments.

## Initial Hearing Process

1. **两造具备** - Both parties present
2. **师听五辞** - Judge hears five types of testimony
3. **五辞简信** - Verify testimony credibility

## Graduated Judgment Process

If testimony is verified:
→ Apply **五刑** (Five Punishments)

If Five Punishments cannot be verified:
→ Apply **五罚** (Five Fines)

If Five Fines are contested:
→ Apply **五过** (Five Mitigations)

## Handling Judicial Errors

**五过之疵** (Flaws in mitigation) include:
- 官狱
- 内狱
- Must verify actual guilt
- Match punishment to offense

## Doubt and Amnesty Protocol

When doubt exists in judgment:
- **五刑之疑有赦** - Doubt in punishment → Grant amnesty
- **五罚之疑有赦** - Doubt in fine → Grant amnesty
- Must carefully investigate (其审克之)

## Expected Outcomes
- Fair adjudication with graduated response
- Reduced wrongful convictions

## Validation
- Verify that both parties (两造具备) were present and all five types of testimony were heard before judgment
- Confirm that the graduated process (五刑 to 五罚 to 五过) was followed and doubt triggered amnesty rather than punishment
- Check that fine rates match the schedule (100率 for 黥辟 through 1000率 for 大辟) when fines replace corporal punishment
