# Divination-Based Decision Procedure

## Overview
This procedure combines consultation with divination to resolve major doubts in governance and important decisions.

## Prerequisites
- Establish trained diviners for turtle shell (卜) and milfoil (筮) methods
- Prepare seven types of signs: rain (雨), clearing (济), obscure (涕), cloudy (雾), overcoming (克), steadfast (贞), changing (悔)

## Decision Process

### Step 1: Identify the Doubt
- Clearly articulate the major question requiring resolution
- Ensure the matter is significant enough to warrant divination

### Step 2: Multi-Source Consultation
Consult in order:
1. **Your own heart/mind** - Internal deliberation
2. **Ministers/Officials (卿士)** - Expert advisors
3. **Common People (庶人)** - Popular sentiment
4. **Divination (卜筮)** - Spiritual guidance

### Step 3: Interpret Results

**Great Harmony (大同)** - All four sources agree:
- Outcome: Personal health and strength, descendants will prosper

**Three Agree, One Disagrees**:
- Ruler + Divination agree: Auspicious
- Ministers + Divination agree: Auspicious
- People + Divination agree: Auspicious

**Divination Disagrees with Humans**:
- Internal matters: Auspicious
- External matters: Inauspicious

**Both Divination Methods Disagree with Humans**:
- Inaction: Auspicious
- Action: Inauspicious

## Decision Rules
- When in doubt, follow the majority opinion
- Divination carries special weight in spiritual matters
- Human consensus matters more for practical affairs

## Expected Outcomes
- Clearer resolution of complex decisions
- Integration of multiple perspectives
- Legitimacy through comprehensive consultation process

## Verification
1. Confirm that all four consultation sources (ruler's heart, ministers, common people, divination) were consulted in proper order.
2. Validate that the consensus level matches the correct interpretation rule (Great Harmony, three-agree, or divination-disagrees scenarios).
3. Check that the decision distinguishes between internal matters (where human consensus weighs more) and external matters (where divination carries special weight).
