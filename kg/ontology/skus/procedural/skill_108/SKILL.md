# Delegation by Comparative Advantage

## Overview
A procedure for assigning responsibilities based on each person's relative strengths, demonstrated by Fan Li's advice to Goujian.

## Steps

1. **Assess each advisor's strengths** - Evaluate what each person does best
2. **Compare capabilities directly** - "For military affairs, Zhong is not as good as Li; for governing the state and winning people's hearts, Li is not as good as Zhong"
3. **Assign primary role to the stronger candidate** - Give civil administration to Zhong, military to Fan Li
4. **Send less critical person as hostage/emissary** - Maintain core leadership at home

## Decision Framework
| Task Type | Assign To |
|-----------|----------|
| Military strategy/operations | Tactical expert |
| Civil administration | Governance expert |
| Diplomatic missions | Expendable but capable |

## Expected Outcomes
- Optimal use of available talent
- Clear division of responsibilities
- Organizational efficiency

## Verification
1. Confirm that each advisor's strengths were directly compared across relevant task domains before assignment.
2. Validate that the strongest candidate for each domain received that primary assignment, not a generalist compromise.
3. Check that less critical roles (emissary, hostage) were assigned to expendable-but-capable individuals rather than core leadership.
