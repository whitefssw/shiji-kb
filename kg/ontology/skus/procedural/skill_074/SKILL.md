# Strategic Decision Under Ill Omens

## Overview
A framework for making strategic decisions when traditional divination or omens suggest failure, but other factors favor action.

## The Dilemma
- Divination shows unfavorable results
- Natural signs appear threatening (storms, etc.)
- But strategic timing and preparation suggest action is needed

## Decision Framework

1. **Assess the Strategic Situation**
   - Is the enemy vulnerable?
   - Is the cause just?
   - Have preparations been made?

2. **Evaluate the Omens Objectively**
   - Are the omens truly relevant to the action?
   - Could fear be clouding judgment?

3. **Consider the Cost of Inaction**
   - Will delay allow the enemy to strengthen?
   - Will momentum be lost?

4. **Make the Decision**
   - If strategic factors strongly favor action, proceed despite omens
   - A strong advisor should advocate for action if the ruler hesitates

5. **Execute Decisively**
   - Do not allow fear to undermine the campaign
   - Commit fully once the decision is made

## Historical Example
Before the Battle of Muye, King Wu's divination showed ill omens and a violent storm arrived. All the lords were afraid, but Taigong strongly urged King Wu to proceed. The result was victory over the Shang dynasty.

## Expected Outcomes
- Victory when strategic factors are favorable
- Avoiding paralysis from superstition
- Building reputation for decisive leadership

## Validation
1. Confirm the strategic assessment was conducted independently of the divination: enemy vulnerability, cause justice, and preparation are all evaluated on their own merits
2. Verify that the decision to proceed was backed by a strong advisor's advocacy, not made recklessly by the leader alone
3. Check that execution was fully committed — no half-measures or hedging that would undermine the campaign once the decision was made
