# Besieged Force Defense

## Overview
Tactical approach for defending against a numerically superior enemy that has surrounded your position. Emphasizes formation discipline, ammunition conservation, and targeting enemy leadership.

## Steps

1. **Form circular defensive position**: Arrange forces in a circle facing outward to defend all directions

2. **Conserve ammunition**: Order soldiers to hold bows drawn but not fire until optimal targets appear

3. **Target enemy commanders**: Use skilled marksmen to specifically target enemy officers and leaders

4. **Maintain composure**: Leaders must display calm confidence regardless of the situation

5. **Continue resistance through nightfall**: Hold position and continue fighting until darkness or reinforcements arrive

6. **Boost morale through action**: Send small forces on aggressive sorties to demonstrate continued fighting capability

## Decision Points
- When to hold fire vs. when to release
- Which enemy targets to prioritize (commanders vs. regular soldiers)
- When to send sorties vs. maintain defensive position

## Expected Outcomes
- Enemy morale degrades from inability to quickly defeat a smaller force
- Enemy leadership losses create command confusion
- Friendly forces may survive until reinforcements arrive or enemy withdraws

## Verification
- Confirm the defensive perimeter covers all approach directions with no exploitable gaps
- Validate that ammunition reserves are being monitored and fire discipline is maintained
- Check that enemy command casualties are disrupting their coordination before committing to sorties
