# Crisis Delegation to Trusted Aides

## Overview
A method for managing complex crises by delegating specific responsibilities to trusted subordinates while maintaining overall strategic control.

## Steps

1. **Assess the crisis scope**
   - Identify all domains requiring attention
   - Determine which issues are most critical
   - Evaluate available resources and personnel

2. **Select trusted delegates**
   - Choose individuals with proven loyalty and competence
   - Match delegate strengths to specific crisis aspects
   - Ensure delegates can work independently

3. **Provide clear instructions**
   - Define specific responsibilities for each delegate
   - Establish communication protocols
   - Set clear boundaries of authority

4. **Maintain strategic oversight**
   - Receive regular reports
   - Make key decisions on matters affecting overall strategy
   - Integrate inputs from multiple delegates

5. **Support delegate actions**
   - Remove obstacles to their effectiveness
   - Provide resources as needed
   - Shield them from political interference

## Example Application
When facing military threats on multiple fronts:
- Delegate northern defense to one trusted general
- Assign logistics coordination to a capable administrator
- Keep diplomatic options open through a trusted envoy

## Expected Outcomes
- Multiple crisis aspects addressed simultaneously
- Efficient use of available talent
- Leader maintains strategic control without being overwhelmed

## Historical Context
Used by various Han emperors when facing simultaneous threats or complex administrative challenges.

## Validation
1. Confirm each delegate has clear, non-overlapping responsibilities with defined boundaries of authority
2. Verify communication protocols are functioning and regular reports are being received from all delegates
3. Check that the leader retains strategic oversight without being pulled into operational details
