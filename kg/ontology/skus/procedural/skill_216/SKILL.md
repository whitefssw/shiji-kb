# Xiongnu Military Tactics

## Overview
The Xiongnu employed highly mobile cavalry tactics emphasizing flexibility, opportunism, and psychological warfare.

## Core Combat Principles

### Engagement Rules
- **有利则进，不利则退** (Advance when advantageous, retreat when disadvantageous)
- **不羞遁走** (No shame in retreat)
- **苟利所在，不知礼义** (Pursue advantage without regard to honor/ceremony)

### Weaponry
- Long-range: Bow and arrow (弓矢)
- Close combat: Sword and spear (刀鋋)

## Tactical Deployment

### Battlefield Behavior
1. **On sighting enemy**: Rush toward advantage "like birds gathering" (如鸟之集)
2. **When defeated**: Scatter and disperse "like clouds" (瓦解云散)
3. **Retreat with dead**: Those who carry fallen comrades receive the deceased's family wealth

### Deception Tactics
- **诱敌** (Lure enemy): Feign weakness to draw enemy into trap
- **示弱** (Show weakness): Hide elite forces, display only weak troops
- **详败** (Feign defeat): Pretend to flee to lead enemy into ambush

## Lunar Warfare
- **月盛壮则攻战** (Attack when moon is full and bright)
- **月亏则退兵** (Withdraw when moon wanes)

## Rewards System
- **Individual incentive**: Captives become personal slaves
- **Spoils distribution**: Captured goods go to the captor
- **Combat motivation**: Each fighter pursues personal gain

## Expected Outcomes
- Highly motivated individual fighters
- Flexible response to battlefield conditions
- Effective use of mobility and deception

## Verification
- Confirm tactical decisions align with the core principle: advance when advantageous, retreat when not
- Validate that deception tactics (luring, feigning weakness, feigning defeat) are coordinated with hidden elite forces
- Check that lunar phase timing is factored into attack and withdrawal planning
