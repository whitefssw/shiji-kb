# Astronomical Observation Method (天官观测法)

This method describes how to observe and interpret celestial phenomena for governance purposes.

## Overview
Celestial observations provide guidance for earthly governance. Changes in the heavens reflect and predict changes on earth.

## Key Celestial Bodies to Observe

### Five Planets (五星)
1. **岁星** - Wood, Spring
   - Location indicates which region will prosper
   - Movement predicts agricultural outcomes

2. **荧惑** - Fire, Summer
   - Indicates warfare and conflict
   - Watch for: 出则有兵，入则兵散

3. **填星** - Earth, Late Summer
   - Indicates stability and territory
   - Movement predicts land changes

4. **太白** - Metal, Autumn
   - Indicates military affairs
   - 出东为德，出西为刑

5. **辰星** - Water, Winter
   - Indicates legal matters and disasters
   - Watch for timing anomalies

## Observation Procedures

### Step 1: Regular Monitoring
- Track planetary positions against 28 constellations (二十八宿)
- Note color, brightness, and movement patterns

### Step 2: Identify Anomalies
- 赢: planet ahead of expected position
- 缩: planet behind expected position
- 犯: planet approaching another celestial body
- 守: planet staying in one position

### Step 3: Interpret Signs
- Good omens: proper alignment, clear colors, regular movement
- Bad omens: irregular movement, dimming, unexpected positions

## Response Framework
- 日变脩德
- 月变省刑
- 星变结和

## Recording Requirements
- Document all observations systematically
- Compare with historical records
- Correlate with earthly events

## Validation
1. Confirm all five planets (岁星, 荧惑, 填星, 太白, 辰星) are being tracked against the 28 constellations with color, brightness, and movement noted
2. Verify anomalies (赢, 缩, 犯, 守) are identified and interpreted correctly against the good/bad omen framework
3. Check that observations are documented systematically and correlated with both historical records and current earthly events
