# De-escalation Through Symbolic Pardon

## Overview
A diplomatic approach to reducing tensions with a powerful subordinate who has shown signs of disloyalty, using symbolic honors rather than punishment. Emperor Wen's handling of Wu Wang's transgressions.

## Steps

1. **Identify the Transgression**
   - Recognize subordinate's failure to meet obligations (e.g., not attending court)
   - Understand underlying grievance (e.g., death of family member)

2. **Avoid Escalatory Actions**
   - Do not imprison or punish envoys
   - Do not issue harsh demands or ultimatums

3. **Acknowledge Grievances Indirectly**
   - Accept explanations even when known to be false
   - Reference wisdom: "Seeing the fish at the bottom of the pool brings bad luck"

4. **Grant Symbolic Honors**
   - Bestow gifts associated with age and respect (staff, stool)
   - These symbolize recognition of status and exemption from duties

5. **Provide Face-Saving Exit**
   - Allow subordinate to maintain dignity
   - Frame non-attendance as privilege of age rather than punishment

6. **Monitor Results**
   - Observe whether tensions decrease
   - Watch for continued or escalated transgressions

## Decision Points
- If symbolic approach fails, may need stronger measures
- Balance mercy against precedent for other subordinates

## Expected Outcomes
- Immediate reduction in tensions
- Subordinate feels respected rather than threatened
- Temporary stability restored

## Limitations
- May only delay rather than resolve underlying conflict
- Does not address root causes of disloyalty

## Verification
1. Confirm that the subordinate's underlying grievance has been identified (even if not directly addressed) before choosing the de-escalation approach.
2. Validate that symbolic honors granted are perceived as genuine respect by the subordinate, not as patronizing gestures.
3. Monitor whether tensions have actually decreased after the symbolic pardon, and prepare stronger measures if transgressions continue or escalate.
