# Governance Transformation Method (from Shang Yang's reforms)

## Overview
A comprehensive approach to transforming state governance through legal and economic reforms.

## Core Reforms

### 1. Mutual Responsibility System
- Organize households into groups of 5-10 (什伍)
- Establish mutual surveillance and collective punishment
- Reward those who report crimes (same as military merit)
- Punish those who conceal crimes (same as surrendering to enemy)

### 2. Family Structure Reform
- Require adult sons to establish separate households
- Penalize families with multiple adult sons living together (double taxation)
- Purpose: Increase household count and tax base

### 3. Merit-Based Reward System
- Military achievement = rank promotion
- Private fighting = criminal punishment
- Agricultural productivity (grain and silk) = tax exemptions
- Commercial failure or laziness = enslavement

### 4. Aristocracy Reform
- Strip hereditary privileges from royal family members without military merit
- Remove names from royal registry if no contribution
- Create new nobility based on achievement

### 5. Standardization
- Unify weights and measures
- Standardize administrative divisions (consolidate into counties)
- Create uniform legal codes

## Implementation Sequence
1. Establish credibility (see credibility-establishment-method)
2. Announce all laws simultaneously
3. Enforce against highest-profile violators first (even crown prince's tutors)
4. Relocate critics to border regions
5. Maintain consistent enforcement for years

## Expected Outcome
A centralized, meritocratic state with strong military and agricultural productivity.

## Validation
1. Verify credibility was established first (see credibility-establishment-method) before announcing reforms — reforms without prior credibility face passive resistance
2. Confirm enforcement against high-profile violators (even crown prince's tutors) has been publicly visible — selective enforcement undermines the entire system
3. Assess whether the merit-based reward system is actually replacing hereditary privilege in practice, not just in proclamation — check the royal registry for names removed
