# Crisis Diplomacy Against Appeasement

A framework for arguing against appeasing aggressive powers that demand recognition of their dominance.

## Steps

1. **Refuse to engage on their terms** - Do not accept the premise that their demands are reasonable
2. **Analyze the true consequences** - Explain what submission actually means:
   - Loss of sovereignty in practice
   - Replacement of your officials with their appointees
   - Your people becoming servants
3. **Cite historical precedents** - Reference examples where submission led to humiliation or destruction
4. **Challenge the logic of fear** - Point out that the adversary is not invincible
5. **Offer concrete alternatives** - Propose coalition building and resistance
6. **Demonstrate personal commitment** - Show willingness to die rather than submit

## Key Arguments
- A ruler who becomes a servant loses everything
- Those who counsel appeasement are thinking of their own positions, not the state
- Even small states can resist if they maintain their dignity and find allies

## Expected Outcomes
- Decision-makers reconsider appeasement
- Coalition resistance forms
- Adversary's momentum checked

## Verification
1. Confirm that the true consequences of submission (loss of sovereignty, replacement of officials, servitude) have been articulated concretely, not abstractly.
2. Validate that concrete coalition-building alternatives have been proposed, with specific potential allies identified.
3. Ensure personal commitment to resistance has been demonstrated to establish credibility with decision-makers.
