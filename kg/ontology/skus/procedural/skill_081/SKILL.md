# Ruler Self-Cultivation Guidelines

## Overview
Principles for rulers to maintain virtue and ensure long-lasting rule, from Zhou Gong's teachings to King Cheng.

## Core Teaching
"As parents who build an estate for a long time, if descendants become arrogant and extravagant and forget this, they will lose their family. How can a son not be careful!"

## Exemplary Models

### Zhong Zong of Yin
- Strict, respectful, and reverent toward Heaven's mandate
- Self-regulated in governing the people
- Trembling with fear, never idle or at ease
- **Result**: Reigned 75 years

### Gao Zong
- Long labored outside the court
- Associated with common people
- Upon taking throne, observed mourning silence for three years
- Never idle or at ease
- Quietly maintained peace in Yin
- No grievances from great or small
- **Result**: Reigned 55 years

### Zu Jia
- Did not unjustly claim the throne
- Long lived among common people outside
- Understood the people's needs
- Protected and showed kindness to common people
- Did not insult widows and widowers
- **Result**: Reigned 33 years

## Warning Example
King Zhou of Yin
- Excessively indulged in pleasure
- Did not care about Heaven or the people
- The people all deserved execution

## Positive Example
King Wen
- Did not have leisure to eat even at midday
- **Result**: Reigned 50 years

## Expected Outcomes
- Long and stable reign
- Content populace
- Preserved ancestral legacy

## Verification
1. Confirm that the ruler's daily practices align with the exemplary models (humility, diligence, reverence) rather than the warning example (indulgence, neglect).
2. Check whether policies reflect concern for both Heaven's mandate and the people's welfare.
3. Validate that the ruler avoids idleness and maintains active governance routines.
