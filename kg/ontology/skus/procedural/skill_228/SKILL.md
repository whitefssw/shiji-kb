# Strategic Court Communication Method

## Overview
A method for advising rulers by presenting options rather than direct confrontation, allowing the superior to feel ownership of decisions.

## Core Principles

1. **Never Publicly Contradict**
   - Avoid face-to-face arguments in court settings
   - Do not directly challenge superiors in front of others

2. **Present Options, Not Demands**
   - "开陈其端，令人主自择" (Present the beginning of ideas, let the ruler choose)
   - Lay out the framework and possibilities
   - Allow the superior to reach the conclusion

3. **Let Others Speak First**
   - Allow colleagues to present initial positions
   - Support or refine after hearing the superior's reaction
   - Position yourself to align with favorable reception

4. **Adapt to Superior's Preferences**
   - If a proposal is rejected, do not argue in court
   - Find alternative approaches or timing
   - Maintain the relationship over winning the immediate point

## Steps

1. **Prepare Your Position**
   - Understand the issue thoroughly
   - Anticipate the superior's concerns

2. **Choose the Right Moment**
   - Private audience when possible
   - When the superior is receptive

3. **Present the Framework**
   - Outline the situation objectively
   - Present multiple possible approaches
   - Highlight implications of each path

4. **Allow the Decision**
   - Let the superior make the final choice
   - Accept the decision gracefully
   - Support implementation fully

## Expected Outcomes
- Maintains harmonious relationships
- Ideas are more likely to be adopted
- Superior feels respected and in control
- Advisor's influence grows over time

## Verification
- Confirm proposals were presented as multiple options rather than single demands
- Validate that the timing and setting (private vs public audience) were appropriate for the sensitivity of the topic
- Check that the superior perceives the final decision as their own choice rather than imposed counsel
