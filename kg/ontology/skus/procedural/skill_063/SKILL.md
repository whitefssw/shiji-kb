## Overview
A framework for evaluating music and dance performances, interpreting their deeper moral and political implications based on the teachings of Ji Zha (延陵季子).

## Steps

1. **Listen to Regional Songs**
   - Observe the foundational character of the music
   - Note whether it expresses diligence without resentment
   - Assess if it shows depth without being trapped in sorrow

2. **Evaluate Emotional Balance**
   - Check for appropriate emotional expression:
     - Joy without excess (乐而不淫)
     - Sorrow without despair (哀而不愁)
     - Grief without oppression

3. **Assess Moral Qualities**
   - Straightness without arrogance (直而不倨)
     - Breadth without ostentation (广而不宣)
   - Giving without waste (施而不费)
   - Taking without greed (取而不贪)

4. **Observe Dance Performances**
   - Note the historical and moral significance
   - Assess whether the performance reflects virtuous leadership
   - Determine if it represents peak moral achievement

5. **Draw Political Conclusions**
   - Connect musical qualities to governance
   - Predict state outcomes based on musical character
   - Identify signs of decline or prosperity

## Decision Points
- If music shows excessive detail or complexity, predict decline
- If music demonstrates balance and restraint, predict stability
- If dance shows complete virtue, recognize it as the highest achievement

## Expected Outcomes
- Accurate assessment of a state's moral and political condition
- Prediction of future prosperity or decline
- Understanding of leadership character through artistic expression

## Validation
1. Confirm emotional balance criteria are assessed: joy without excess (乐而不淫), sorrow without despair (哀而不愁)
2. Verify moral qualities are evaluated: straightness without arrogance (直而不倨), breadth without ostentation (广而不宣), giving without waste (施而不费)
3. Check that political conclusions are drawn — musical qualities are connected to governance predictions about prosperity or decline
