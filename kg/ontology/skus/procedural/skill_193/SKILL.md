# Rebellion Mobilization Process

## Overview
A systematic approach to mobilizing military forces for a coordinated rebellion, as executed by Wu Wang Pi during the Seven States Rebellion.

## Steps

1. **Establish Command Structure**
   - Designate supreme commander (Wu Wang led personally at age 62)
   - Appoint youngest eligible family members to frontline positions (age 14)

2. **Define Mobilization Parameters**
   - Set upper age limit (matching commander's age)
   - Set lower age limit (matching youngest participant)
   - All men within range are subject to conscription

3. **Coordinate with Allied Forces**
   - Send envoys to allied states (Minyue, Dongyue)
   - Request troop contributions and coordination

4. **Issue Proclamations**
   - Distribute written declarations to all allied princes
   - State grievances and justification for action
   - Outline strategic objectives and coordination plans

5. **Establish Reward Structure**
   - Create clear incentive hierarchy:
     - Capturing enemy general: 5000 jin gold, 10,000 household fief
     - Capturing lieutenant general: 3000 jin gold, 5000 household fief
     - Lower ranks: proportionally reduced rewards
   - Rewards for cities/troops that surrender
   - All rewards double standard military regulations

6. **Secure Financial Resources**
   - Declare availability of funds across the realm
   - Promise immediate payment for services

## Expected Outcomes
- Mass mobilization of eligible population
- Coordinated multi-front offensive
- Clear incentive structure for participants

## Verification
1. Confirm that the command structure and conscription age parameters are established before issuing mobilization orders.
2. Validate that allied states (e.g., Minyue, Dongyue) have received envoys and confirmed troop contributions before coordinated action begins.
3. Ensure the reward structure is published and financial resources are declared available to maintain credibility with participants.
