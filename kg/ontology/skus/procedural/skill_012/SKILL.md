# Leadership Succession Protocol

A procedure for managing power transitions between rulers based on ancient Chinese succession practices.

## Overview
This procedure outlines the formal process for transferring authority from one ruler to the successor.

## Steps

1. **Designation of Successor**
   - Current ruler recommends successor to heaven (荐于天)
   - Successor serves as heir apparent (嗣)
   - Period of joint governance may occur

2. **Mourning Period**
   - Observe three-year mourning period (三年丧)
   - Administrative functions continue
   - Successor prepares for assumption

3. **Formal Abdication Gesture**
   - Successor offers position to deceased ruler's son
   - Withdraws to separate location (辟居)
   - Demonstrates humility and propriety

4. **Popular Validation**
   - Regional leaders (诸侯) choose whom to support
   - Those seeking legitimacy gather at chosen leader
   - Popular support confirms succession

5. **Formal Assumption**
   - Accept position if support is clear
   - Face south to receive homage (南面朝天下)
   - Establish state name and clan designation

## Decision Points
- If predecessor's son lacks support, successor may assume power
- If successor lacks established reputation, predecessor's heir may prevail

## Success Factors
- Length of service under previous ruler
- Established relationships with regional leaders
- Demonstrated virtue and capability

## Expected Outcomes
- Peaceful power transition
- Legitimate authority established
- Continued governance stability

## Validation
- Verify that the successor was formally recommended to heaven (荐于天) and served as heir apparent before assuming power
- Confirm that the three-year mourning period was observed and the abdication gesture was performed
- Check that regional leaders (诸侯) demonstrated clear popular support before the formal assumption of authority
