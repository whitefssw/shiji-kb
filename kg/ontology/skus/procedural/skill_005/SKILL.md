# Personnel Appointment Consultation

A systematic process for appointing government officials through advisor consultation, merit evaluation, and role matching.

## Overview
This procedure ensures qualified officials are appointed through collective wisdom, matching individual strengths to specific governmental functions.

## Steps

1. **Identify Vacancy or Need**
   - Determine which position needs filling
   - Define the specific responsibilities and requirements

2. **Consult Senior Advisors**
   - Present the question to the Four Peaks (四岳) or council
   - Ask: "Who can excel in this position?"
   - Allow open discussion and recommendations

3. **Evaluate Candidates**
   - Consider multiple nominees
   - Assess each candidate's:
     - Past achievements
     - Relevant skills
     - Character and reputation

4. **Allow Self-Declination**
   - Permit candidates to decline
   - Allow candidates to recommend others
   - Consider their reasoning

5. **Match Skills to Roles**
   - Water management → Appoint as Minister of Works (司空)
   - Agriculture expertise → Appoint as Minister of Agriculture (后稷)
   - Education/moral teaching → Appoint as Minister of Instruction (司徒)
   - Legal expertise → Appoint as Minister of Justice (士)
   - Craft management → Appoint as Director of Works (共工)
   - Nature management → Appoint as Minister of Forests (虞)
   - Ritual expertise → Appoint as Minister of Rites (秩宗)
   - Music education → Appoint as Director of Music (典乐)
   - Communication skills → Appoint as Director of Communications (纳言)

6. **Confirm Appointment**
   - Announce the decision formally
   - State specific expectations
   - Encourage the appointee

7. **Assign Supporting Staff**
   - Allow appointment of assistants
   - Ensure adequate resources

## Expected Outcomes
- Competent officials in appropriate positions
- Balanced government structure
- Clear accountability
- Efficient administration

## Validation
- Verify that each appointee's skills match the specific ministerial function they were assigned to
- Confirm that the Four Peaks (四岳) council was consulted and multiple candidates were evaluated before selection
- Check that appointees were given the opportunity to decline and that supporting staff were assigned
